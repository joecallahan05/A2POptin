Message Flow & Call‑to‑Action

Program Description

The Chickasaw Nation Enhanced Citizen Services (ECS) SMS program uses Twilio’s A2P 10DLC platform to deliver strictly informational text messages—form submission confirmations, appointment reminders, status updates, and other service notifications—to enrolled citizens.

1. How End‑Users Consent to Receive Messages

Direct Opt‑In Link:
https://joecallahan05.github.io/ChickasawECS/main.html

Step‑by‑Step Flow

  1. User navigates to chickasaw.net → clicks “Enhanced Citizen Services.”
  2. The link redirects to our hosted opt‑in form:
    https://joecallahan05.github.io/ChickasawECS/main.html
  3. On that page, the citizen reads the intro paragraph:
    “By opting in below, you agree to receive informational SMS messages for the services you select. Messages include form submission confirmations, appointment reminders, and status updates. All messages are strictly informational and support access to tribal services and programs.”
  4. Citizen enters their phone number (pattern‑validated).
  5. Citizen checks at least one service checkbox (e.g. “Pathways To Success,” “Diabetes Care Center,” etc.)
  6. Citizen clicks Submit.
  7. JavaScript immediately redirects user to the “Thank You” page:
    https://joecallahan05.github.io/ChickasawECS/ThankYouOpt-in.html

3. Organization Identification

Every initial SMS clearly identifies the Chickasaw Nation ECS program. Example:
“Chickasaw Nation ECS: Thank you for submitting your form. We’ll keep you updated here. Reply HELP for info, STOP to end.”

4. Opt‑In Language & Fees

On the web form, the checkbox label reads:
“By checking here, I agree to receive informational SMS messages from Chickasaw Nation ECS. Message & data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help, STOP to unsubscribe.”

No subscription fees beyond standard carrier data/SMS charges.

5. Privacy Policy

6. Opt‑Out & Help Instructions

Each SMS includes the footer:

“Reply HELP for help, STOP to unsubscribe.”
